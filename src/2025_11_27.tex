A woman has me rethinking some of my poem-writing workflows.

Karen Uhlenbeck, she had a unique rad note-taking process.

She solved math problems of course, as jobs in her long career.

And when she got stuck, she threw her working notes in a fire.

Keeping only the solved notes, she moved on to something new,

Then sometimes return to unsolved problems with a fresh point of view.

People take notes at work so they can pick up where they left off.

Karen's process is different, she burns it if it's not clear enough.

This works in her favour because later, there's no past anchoring.

I believe this fantastic approach could help my drafting.
