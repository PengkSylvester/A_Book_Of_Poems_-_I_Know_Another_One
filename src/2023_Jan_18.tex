\marginnote[1mm]{\textit{When punishment for what people say becomes widespread, people stop saying what they really think and instead say whatever is needed to thrive in the social environment. Thus, limits on speech become limits on sincerity.} \newthought{- Gurwinder Bhogal}}

I was on my own, jejely driving going along the lake
When a Corolla horned twice, then bashed me by mistake 
The bastard hit me and tried to run, 
my heart started to beat faster; let us have this fun.
I kept my eyes on his licence plate as him I chased
My car moved like knife through butter as I drove in haste
After a mile, the guy took no while to park his blue car
He got out and started running thinking he could get far
On your marks, get set, go. I didn't know I had foam spit
I chased this motherfucker like Bad Boys's Will Smith
With every breath, every step, I could taste his neck
I felt mad; stark raving. This man had made my car a wreck
So after I'd drowned his body, I went back to gather my sense
Then a lady stopped my car; asked for my driver's licence
I smiled, and told her ``I'm sorry officer, I left it at home''
She said \textit{``wait here''}, and then left me on my own
That was when I woke up.
