\marginnote[1mm]{\textit{}}

Real poets don't need a catchy hook nor a hot beat
to write. If you have something to say then just speak.
Alright, sure, it's difficult... Except, really, it's not.
You can be sonically pleasant to pass a proverb you've got.
The valuables I want from you, aren't the ones that gleem and shine,
I want the ones \textbf{in} your head, messages you could put in rhyme.
Probably every time you write a piece, it should say something.
Run me them jewels, this ain't a game, it's a damn robbery.
