\marginnote[1mm]{\textit{A dead thing can go with the stream, but only a living thing can go against it.} \newthought{- G.K. Chesterton}}

``If our young men miscarry in their first enterprises, 
they lose all heart, the young merchant catastrophises.
If the finest genius studies at one of our colleges, 
and is not installed in office within one year afterwards 
in the cities or suburbs of Boston or New York, 
it seems to his friends and to himself that he is fucked.
He resigns to be disheartened and complain all his life. 
A sturdy lad from Hampshire or Vermont who finds a wife
and in turn tries professions; teams it, farms it, peddles,
keeps a school, edits a paper, goes to Congress, settles,
buys a village, preaches, teaches, and so forth, in years,
and just always, like a cat, falls on his feet, is clear-
ly worth a hundred times of the saddened city dolls.
He walks abreast with his days, and feels no shame at all
in not ‘studying a profession’, for he does not postpone
his life, but lives, self-reliantly takes chances one by one.”
\vspace{3mm}\textsc{- Paraphrased from Ralph Waldo Emerson}
