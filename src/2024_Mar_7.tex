\textit{[TO MY FATHER]}
\vspace{0.4cm}

Onowu sir, I pray you're having a good day.
\vspace{0.4cm}
I just received the three AirPeace tickets you sent from Uncle Bsil Kogolo.\ I’m travelling on Tuesday, March 12th, from
Abuja to Asaba.
\vspace{0.4cm}
On Wednesday morning, I intend to meet with Dr Isaac Wilkie, the Commissioner for Water Resources, and Jamani
Ejiro, the Commissioner for Environment, both at the State Secretariat Building, to discuss acquiring this concession.\ I
place myself unreservedly in your hands.\ If there is any point which I have not made clear, I pray you question me
about it.
\vspace{0.4cm}
Similar pitch letters to the one that I sent to you yesterday, have today been sent to Uncle Ekene Abiamuwe, and Uncle
Shedrack Oyibo.\ I want to see the landscape, understand the facts quickly, and make the accurate judgments whether to
go ahead and reach out to more potential investors.
\vspace{0.4cm}
Grandma Elizabeth Abiamuwe, and Caroline “Mydear” Abiamuwe are traveling on Friday, March 15th, from Abuja to
Agbor.\ My grandmother insists that it would make her glad to spend a week with me, in these her twilight years, so I
will travel to Agbor for seven days to go absorb everything she intends to teach me.
\vspace{0.4cm}
I haven’t forgotten that I must attend my American Embassy appointment on Monday, April 15th, so I intend to return
to Abuja by Friday, April 12th.\ I am committed to collecting the requisite knowledge for this Delta State Dam project
within the 31 days that I am in Delta, and I trust God that we will achieve alignment.
\vspace{0.4cm}
I’ll see you soon, days after this letter is sent, sincerely
\vspace{0.4cm}
Your dutiful humble Son.

\end{fullwidth}
