The name "honeymoon" traces back to 1500's Europe.
Back then, people would drink mead, made from honey syrup,
The moon in june looked like that mead when it was full and amber,
Because the moon was angled low in the sky to get that brownish glamour.
That tradition of calling the first month of marriage the "honeymoon" 
Is tied to this Mead Moon, and their customs of marrying in June.
Our honeymoon is about us celebrating our sweet love and bond.
And our every June anniversary will have a Honey Moon on that month.
