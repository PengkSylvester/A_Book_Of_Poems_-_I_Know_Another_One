\marginnote[1mm]{\textit{Man can do what he wills but he cannot will what he wills.} \newthought{- Arthur Schopenhauer}}

Contrary to determinism, free will isn't an illusion

We make decisions and predictions every day we're living

Even if you're always right and so wise, look at the comparison

Others make choices that aren't right, despite all your computing

People cannot be pre-programmed to all arrive at a destination

And even WHEN you're ready for the unpredictable to happen

Something somewhere might surprise you and fuck up your whole mission

Determinism sees all history as one chained chemical reaction
