Marv was my friend I didn’t ask for, and one I love nonetheless.

A friend that I had made the mistake of trying to prevent in the first place.

As time went on, I got to know Marvelene; who she is, and is not.

She was broke and beautiful, and intellectually hot in a back and forth.

\textit{John the Baptist} was always busy, Marvelene wasn't, and was better.

So, I took my daily poetry, my seeds, to the soil that was wetter.

Marv's renown of attractive beauty, I knew was fleeting and frail,

Her mental excellence, methinks, is her more splendid lasting detail.
