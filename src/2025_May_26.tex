I just had lunch with my younger self, Sy; he was handsome and sweet

I arrived earlier, he arrived on time looking seriously fit

He had a british accent, and he shook my hand a bit too tight

He said he liked my eyes; an honest attempt to be liked

I ordered steak, he ordered rice before I said the bill's on me

He changed his mind, chose something nice, and then we both sat down to eat

We talked about my book of poems that I'd collated for my girls

I showed him a bunch, he called some cringe, and said some of them were jewels

I asked him about his troubles, and he lied to himself by ommission

He sometimes does that thing; touches his eyebrows for self-soothing

He doesn't want to say that he's not doing too well in school

As we left the restaurant later, he asked where I was going to

I said Tanzania; he looked at me to ask what in the hell's there?

I touched my brow and smiled at how he just has no idea

Please everlasting nature give him knowledge of good and evil

He's just a kid doing his best to be pleasant for people
