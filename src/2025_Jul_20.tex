\marginnote[1mm]{\textit{Fairy tales do not tell children that dragons exist. Children already know that dragons exist. Fairy tales tell children that dragons can be killed.} \newthought{- G.~K.~Chesterton}}


What surprises me is that the world is full of kind people


Instead of everyone out for themselves


When I was small, I used to be scared of strangers, because people were dangerous around me


They would physically harm me for no reason I could think of


So, I was always careful around people because I expected them to hurt me


If a look didn't come with a smile, I'd get scared. Because that had used to come before a whooping.


Then when I started travelling, I expected people to be thieves, or killers, or something


Or at the very least neutral


I cannot explain how surprising it is to be finding people who are genuinely kind


People that give without wanting anything


Their kindness isn't a trap for evil


They have enough to give, and care enough to give.
